Biography

Ekramul Haque is an environmental researcher and journalist based in Khulna, Bangladesh. He is currently pursuing a Master of Science in Forestry at Khulna University, where his academic work focuses on land use and land cover dynamics, environmental change, and conservation policy.

His ongoing graduate research investigates the spatiotemporal dynamics of land use and land cover in the Sundarbans Ecologically Critical Area (2000–2025), using remote sensing and GIS techniques. Through tools such as Google Earth Engine and ArcGIS Pro, he conducts detailed spatial analysis and develops policy-relevant insights to support sustainable forest management.

He completed his Bachelor of Science (Honours) in Forestry from Khulna University, following outstanding academic achievements in both secondary and higher secondary education with GPA 5.00. His undergraduate dissertation focused on forest cover change in Madhupur National Park (2015–2024).

Beyond academia, Ekramul is an active environmental journalist who has contributed extensively to national and regional media outlets. His journalistic work translates complex scientific findings into accessible narratives that inform public discourse on climate change, biodiversity loss, and environmental governance.
