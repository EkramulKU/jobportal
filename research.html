Graduate Thesis

Ongoing Research MSc Forestry · 2024–Present

Spatiotemporal Dynamics of Land Use and Land Cover in the Sundarbans Ecologically Critical Area (2000–2025)

Khulna University Sundarbans, Bangladesh 2024 – Present

This research investigates the 25-year trajectory of land use and land cover (LULC) change within the Sundarbans Ecologically Critical Area — the world's largest mangrove forest. Using multi-temporal satellite imagery from Landsat and Sentinel-2, processed through Google Earth Engine, the study quantifies habitat transformation, tracks vegetation loss, and models future land cover scenarios. The findings are expected to provide evidence-based policy recommendations for sustainable forest management and mangrove conservation.

Undergraduate Dissertation

✅ Completed BSc Forestry · 2024

Forest Cover Change Detection in Madhupur National Park, Bangladesh (2015–2024)

This dissertation employed multi-date Landsat imagery to detect and quantify forest cover change in Madhupur National Park over a nine-year period. The study applied supervised classification techniques and change detection algorithms to map deforestation, degradation, and recovery patterns. Results revealed significant canopy loss in peripheral buffer zones due to agricultural encroachment and infrastructure development.

  • Landsat 8 & 9 OLI imagery (2015, 2018, 2021, 2024)
  • Maximum likelihood and Random Forest classification
  • Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI) trend analysis
  • Accuracy assessment with confusion matrix (overall accuracy >90%)
  • Land cover transition matrix and statistical analysis in R
  • Policy recommendations for buffer zone management
